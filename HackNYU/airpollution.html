Air pollution

Industrial Gases
Industrial Gases

The type of pollutants generated from industrial emissions are closely related to the industrial purpose of each factories. These days, we can often see enormous clouds of smoke billowed out of the chimney around the globe, transporting pollutants into the atmosphere.

Fuel Combustion
Fuel Combustion

The combustion process of fuel (coal, oil and natural gas, etc.) produces a significant amount of pollutants that are being transported into the atmosphere. When fuel is burned, lots of dust is produced, as well as pollution substances (ex. carbon monoxide, carbon dioxide, and nitrogen oxides).

Automobile Exhaust
Automobile Exhaust

The exhaust gas from automobiles, ships, and airplanes is the main source of air pollution. As we use these vehicles, the combustion of internal engines produce these exhaust gases (ie. carbon monoxide, hydrocarbons).
